Homicide:

Over the past nine years, more than 800 people were killed on the streets of San Antonio.

  • Written by: Nolan Hicks
  • Developed by: Nolan Hicks and Joseph Kokenge
  • Stories by: San Antonio Express-News staff reporters
  • Homicide data updated and maintained by: Jacob Beltran

Every homicide victim has a story. They were someone's child, someone's parent. Some were bystanders: the young, the old, the hard worker who happened to be in the wrong place at the wrong time. Others were participants: criminals, gangsters, drug dealers, pimps. Some were shot out on the streets, others were killed in their homes. Every one of their deaths impacted their families, their friends and their neighborhoods.

From January 2007 through to the present day, reporters at the Express-News have tracked every homicide in the city limits, publishing hundreds of stories along the way. This ongoing project aims to combine those records and stories, transforming them into a standing community resource that provides a record of those lost and serve as an exhibit of the cumulative impact of violence in our community.

Each weekend, Express-News reporters update the homicides from the past week. In addition, reporters are in constant contact with SAPD officials to verify details related to both confirmed and suspected homicides.
