Homicide:

Over seven years, more than 800 people were killed on the streets of San Antonio.

  • Written by: Nolan Hicks
  • Developed by: Nolan Hicks and Joseph Kokenge
  • Stories by San Antonio Express-News staff reporters

Every homicide victim has a story. They were someone's child, someone's parent. Some were innocent bystanders, the young, the old, the hard worker who happened to be in the wrong place at the wrong time. Others were criminals, gangsters, drug dealers or pimps. Some were shot out on the streets, others were killed in their home. Every one of their deaths impacted their families and their communities.

From January 2007 through June 2013, reporters at the Express-News kept track of every homicide in the city limits, publishing hundreds of stories along the way. The project aims to combine the records and stories and transform them into a standing community resource, that will serve as a record of who were lost and as an exhibit of the cumulative impact of violence in our community.
